Item 1: A patient who just underwent thyroidectomy develops audible stridor and restlessness. The pulse oximetry is 94%. Which action should the nurse take first?
  • A. Check the surgical dressing for signs of hemorrhage.
  • B. Prepare for immediate tracheostomy at the bedside.
  • C. Notify the surgeon regarding the change in respiratory status.
  • D. Explain to the patient that some swelling is normal after surgery.

Correct Answer: B

Prometric Trap: Delay disguised as thoroughness (Option A) or Authority Trap (Option C).

Safety Filter: Airway before Diagnosis/Documentation. Stridor indicates an immediate airway emergency; waiting for a surgeon or checking dressings causes a fatal delay.

Item 2: A nurse is preparing to administer Digoxin to a patient. The patient’s heart rate is 54 bpm, and the patient reports seeing "yellow halos" around the lights. What is the priority nursing action?
  • A. Document the heart rate and the patient's subjective visual report.
  • B. Hold the medication and continue to monitor the patient every 15 minutes.
  • C. Administer the dose and then call the physician to report the visual changes.
  • D. Withhold the dose immediately.

Correct Answer: D

Prometric Trap: Documentation Trap (Option A) and Monitoring instead of Intervening (Option B).

Safety Filter: Hold unsafe meds, don’t wait. The nurse has the autonomy to withhold an unsafe medication based on clinical evidence of toxicity without needing a prior order to "stop."

Item 3: A patient 2 hours post-laparotomy has a blood pressure of 88/50 mmHg and a heart rate of 120 bpm. The patient is pale and diaphoretic. What is the nurse's first action?
  • A. Increase the IV fluid rate as per standing protocol for hypotension.
  • B. Perform a full head-to-toe assessment to find the source of bleeding.
  • C. Re-check the blood pressure in 15 minutes to confirm the trend.
  • D. Call the rapid response team to evaluate the patient's status.

Correct Answer: A

Prometric Trap: Complexity Trap (Option B) - trying to find the "why" instead of fixing the "what."

Safety Filter: Hypotension = Fluids First. Stability overrides the need for a comprehensive diagnosis in the acute phase.

Item 4: A nurse enters the room of a patient on droplet precautions and finds the patient on the floor, unresponsive and not breathing. What should the nurse do first?
  • A. Leave the room to find the correct PPE kit and N95 mask.
  • B. Begin chest compressions immediately while calling for help.
  • C. Activate the emergency response system and wait for the code team.
  • D. Check the patient's wristband to confirm identity before starting CPR.

Correct Answer: B

Prometric Trap: Overuse of PPE steps (Option A). In many exams, safety protocols are tested, but life-saving stability comes first in "dead on floor" scenarios.

Safety Filter: Address patient stability first. An unresponsive, non-breathing patient requires immediate intervention that outweighs procedural perfection.

Item 5: During a dressing change for a post-operative abdominal wound, the nurse observes a large amount of bright red blood spurting from the incision site. What is the priority?
  • A. Apply firm direct pressure to the site using sterile gauze.
  • B. Run to the nursing station to call the surgeon immediately.
  • C. Obtain a sterile culture swab to check for underlying infection.
  • D. Document the estimated blood loss in the clinical notes.

Correct Answer: A

Prometric Trap: Sterility Obsession or Documentation Trap.

Safety Filter: Control bleeding first. In active hemorrhage, "Pressure" is the safe action; "Sterility" is secondary to "Survival."

Item 6: A patient with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is receiving oxygen at 2L/min via nasal cannula. The patient suddenly becomes agitated, confused, and the SpO2 drops to 82%. What is the nurse’s immediate action?
  • A. Increase the oxygen flow rate to 4L/min and monitor for CO2 narcosis.
  • B. Call the respiratory therapist to perform an arterial blood gas (ABG) draw.
  • C. Wait for the physician’s order before changing the prescribed oxygen flow.
  • D. Encourage the patient to perform pursed-lip breathing exercises.

Correct Answer: A

Prometric Trap: Authority/Waiting Trap (Option C) or Complexity Trap (Option B).

Safety Filter: Oxygen is Nurse-Initiated in emergencies. Hypoxia kills faster than CO2 retention. Act before the ABG results or orders arrive.

Item 7: While performing a routine check on a patient admitted for a head injury, the nurse notes the patient’s Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) has shifted from 14 to 11. The patient is now somnolent but arousable to pain. What is the priority?
  • A. Re-assess the GCS in one hour to determine if this is a temporary fluctuation.
  • B. Perform a full cranial nerve assessment and document the findings.
  • C. Notify the neurosurgeon immediately of the acute decline.
  • D. Provide a quiet environment to allow the patient to rest.

Correct Answer: C

Prometric Trap: Reassessment Loop (Option A) or Documentation Trap (Option B).

Safety Filter: Acute Neuro Change = Escalate. A 3-point drop in GCS is a neurosurgical emergency. Monitoring "trends" during an acute drop is a fail marker.

Item 8: A patient presents with severe epigastric pain radiating to the back, nausea, and a rigid, board-like abdomen. The nurse suspects acute pancreatitis or perforation. Which action takes precedence?
  • A. Maintain NPO status and prepare for emergency surgical consultation.
  • B. Administer a prescribed oral antacid to relieve the epigastric pain.
  • C. Assist the patient to a supine position for a thorough physical exam.
  • D. Wait for the serum amylase and lipase results to confirm the diagnosis.

Correct Answer: A

Prometric Trap: Diagnosis Fixation (Option D) or Comfort Trap (Option B).

Safety Filter: Acute Abdomen = Protect & Report. Rigid abdomen implies a surgical emergency. NPO is the safety filter; feeding or waiting for labs is a "Delay Trap."

A patient with acute asthma exacerbation has high-pitched wheezing. After 15 minutes, the nurse notes the chest is silent on auscultation and the patient is drowsy.
What is the priority nursing action?
A. Administer a prescribed PRN sedative to reduce anxiety.
B. Prepare for immediate endotracheal intubation.
C. Request a repeat peak flow meter reading to confirm status.
D. Document "silent chest" and continue to monitor vitals.
Key: B
Trap: Delay (reassessing) & Complexity (wait for labs).
Filter: Airway before Diagnosis. A silent chest in asthma is a pre-arrest emergency.
During a shift change, the nurse finds an IV bag of Potassium Chloride (KCl) infusing via gravity at a rapid rate, not on a pump as ordered.
What must the nurse do first?
A. Call the physician to report the error and request a stat ECG.
B. Check the patient's peripheral IV site for infiltration.
C. Stop the infusion immediately using the roller clamp.
D. Document the incident in the hospital’s error-reporting system.
Key: C
Trap: Documentation Trap (D) and Authority Trap (A).
Filter: Hold unsafe meds, don’t wait. Stop the harm before reporting it.
A patient complains of sudden crushing chest pain and diaphoresis. The ECG shows ST-segment elevation.
Which action is the nurse's priority?
A. Explain the importance of deep breathing to the patient.
B. Administer oxygen and prescribed sublingual nitroglycerin.
C. Call the laboratory to check the previous troponin level.
D. Wait for the cardiology fellow to arrive before initiating treatment.
Key: B
Trap: Comfort Trap (A) and Wait for Orders (D).
Filter: Chest pain = Basic Intervention. Time is muscle; stabilize first.
A post-craniotomy patient has a sudden increase in urine output of 500mL in 1 hour and is complaining of extreme thirst.
What is the safest nursing action?
A. Increase oral fluid intake to match the output.
B. Notify the physician and check urine specific gravity.
C. Document the output and reassess after the next hour.
D. Restrict fluids to prevent further intracranial pressure.
Key: B
Trap: Reassessment Loop (C).
Filter: Acute neuro/fluid change = Escalate/Act. Suspected Diabetes Insipidus.
A nurse is caring for a patient with suspected Tuberculosis (TB). The patient needs to be transported to the radiology department for a CXR.
What is the priority action for transport?
A. Put an N95 respirator on the patient during transport.
B. Place a surgical mask on the patient.
C. Cancel the X-ray until the patient is no longer infectious.
D. Notify the radiology staff to wear N95 masks while the patient is in the room.
Key: B
Trap: Overuse of PPE (A) - Patients never wear N95s.
Filter: Protocol Mastery. Safety Filter: Contain the source (Surgical mask).
A patient with a suspected bowel obstruction has a rigid, tender abdomen and is vomiting greenish fluid.
What action should the nurse prioritize?
A. Insert a nasogastric (NG) tube and attach to low suction.
B. Provide a light liquid diet to keep the patient hydrated.
C. Administer an oral laxative to clear the obstruction.
D. Assist the patient into a high-Fowler’s position for comfort.
Key: A
Trap: Comfort Trap (D) and Danger Trap (B/C).
Filter: Acute Abdomen = Protect (Decompress).
An elderly patient receiving aggressive IV fluid resuscitation for sepsis develops crackles in the lung bases and a respiratory rate of 28/min.
What is the nurse's first response?
A. Document the breath sounds and continue the IV fluids.
B. Slow the IV infusion rate to a "keep-vein-open" (KVO) rate.
C. Administer the next scheduled dose of antibiotics.
D. Provide the patient with an extra pillow for comfort.
Key: B
Trap: Documentation Trap (A) and Comfort Trap (D).
Filter: Symptoms > Numbers. Pulmonary edema takes priority over the sepsis fluid protocol.
A neonate is born with a heart rate of 90 bpm and gasping respirations.
What should the nurse do first?
A. Dry and stimulate the infant to cry.
B. Administer blow-by oxygen via tubing.
C. Begin positive pressure ventilation (PPV).
D. Perform a full Apgar score assessment.
Key: C
Trap: Delay (A) and Monitoring Trap (D).
Filter: Stability First. HR < 100 in neonate = Vent Support immediately.
A diabetic patient is found unresponsive, diaphoretic, and cold to the touch. The glucose monitor is being calibrated and is unavailable.
What is the priority?
A. Wait for the lab to draw a formal venous glucose level.
B. Administer IV Dextrose 50% immediately.
C. Apply a warm blanket to treat the cold skin.
D. Check the patient's pupils and GCS score.
Key: B
Trap: Diagnosis Trap (A) - waiting for a number.
Filter: Symptoms > Numbers. Treat "Deadly Low" (Hypo) rather than wait for proof.
A patient with dementia is trying to pull out their central venous catheter. The family refuses the use of any restraints.
What is the safest nursing action?
A. Comply with the family's wishes and document the risk.
B. Assign a 1:1 sitter to provide constant observation.
C. Administer a heavy sedative to keep the patient still.
D. Tell the family they must take the patient home immediately.
Key: B
Trap: Language Trap (A) - following "family rights" at the cost of safety.
Filter: Safety > Debate. Prevent the injury immediately.
A patient's abdominal incision eviscerates, with a loop of bowel visible.
The nurse's immediate action is to:
A. Use a sterile gloved hand to push the bowel back in.
B. Cover the bowel with sterile gauze moistened with normal saline.
C. Leave the room to get an emergency surgical kit.
D. Check the last recorded temperature for signs of infection.
Key: B
Trap: Delay (C) or Danger Action (A).
Filter: Protect & Report. Moisten to prevent necrosis.
A patient’s laboratory results show a serum potassium of 6.8 mEq/L. The patient is currently asymptomatic but the ECG monitor shows peaked T-waves.
Which intervention must the nurse implement first?
A. Repeat the blood draw to rule out lab error or hemolysis.
B. Administer prescribed Calcium Gluconate IV.
C. Encourage the patient to increase oral fluid intake.
D. Document the lab value and notify the dietitian to limit bananas.
Key: B
Trap: Delay (A) and Nutrition focus (D).
Filter: Abnormal + Unstable = Intervene. Calcium gluconate protects the heart before the potassium is even lowered.
A nurse is assisting a patient with a history of syncope to the bathroom. The patient suddenly becomes pale, sweaty, and says, "I feel like I'm going to pass out."
What is the priority action?
A. Call for another staff member to bring a wheelchair.
B. Perform an immediate blood glucose check.
C. Ease the patient to the floor and protect their head.
D. Instruct the patient to take slow, deep breaths.
Key: C
Trap: Teaching (D) and Waiting (A).
Filter: Prevent fall immediately. Do not leave the patient to find equipment.
A patient who is 12 hours post-abdominal surgery is crying and rating their pain as 10/10. The vitals are BP 150/90, HR 110, RR 24.
What should the nurse do first?
A. Re-assess the pain score using a different scale.
B. Explain that frequent opioid use can lead to constipation.
C. Check the MAR and administer the prescribed analgesic.
D. Wait for the surgeon to round before giving medication.
Key: C
Trap: Over-assessing (A) and Long Explanations (B).
Filter: Treat pain if no contraindication. Do not let "education" delay relief.
The unit is extremely busy. The RN needs to complete several tasks.
Which task is most appropriate to delegate to an Unlicensed Assistive Personnel (UAP)?
A. Feeding a patient who had a stroke 2 hours ago.
B. Measuring the output from a Foley catheter.
C. Assessing the skin integrity of a new admission.
D. Providing discharge teaching to a patient with a new ostomy.
Key: B
Trap: Over-delegation (A/C/D).
Filter: RN keeps safety-critical actions (Assessment, Teaching, Unstable patients).
A patient with a chest tube suddenly develops severe respiratory distress, diminished breath sounds on the affected side, and tracheal deviation.
What is the priority nursing action?
A. Check the chest tube drainage system for leaks.
B. Notify the physician immediately for a needle decompression.
C. Document the deviation and monitor the SpO2.
D. Clamp the chest tube to see if the symptoms improve.
Key: B
Trap: Documentation (C) and Technical fixation (A).
Filter: Airway/Breathing before diagnosis. Tracheal deviation is a late, fatal sign of Tension Pneumothorax.
A patient’s heart rate drops to 38 bpm. The patient is dizzy and has a BP of 80/40 mmHg.
Which action is the nurse's first priority?
A. Start an IV infusion of normal saline.
B. Administer prescribed Atropine IV push.
C. Perform a 12-lead ECG to determine the rhythm.
D. Record the rhythm strip in the patient's chart.
Key: B
Trap: Complexity/Analysis (C) and Documentation (D).
Filter: Symptomatic Bradycardia = Act now. Treating the rate is the safety filter.
A nurse is calling a physician to report a patient's deteriorating condition.
To ensure the safest outcome, when should the nurse use the SBAR format?
A. While the patient is actively arresting.
B. After the patient has been stabilized.
C. Before performing any life-saving interventions.
D. Only during routine morning rounds.
Key: B
Trap: Over-reporting (A/C).
Filter: Report after stabilizing. Communication never precedes resuscitation in Prometric.
A patient begins having a tonic-clonic seizure while sitting in a chair.
What is the priority action?
A. Insert a padded tongue blade into the patient's mouth.
B. Restrain the patient's arms to prevent injury.
C. Ease the patient to the floor and turn them on their side.
D. Run to the medication room to get Diazepam.
Key: C
Trap: Fear of acting/Leaving (D) and Danger action (A/B).
Filter: First safe step counts. Airway protection (side-lying) is the filter.
Following a carotid endarterectomy, a patient’s neck circumference is increasing rapidly and they are struggling to breathe.
The nurse should first:
A. Call the rapid response team.
B. Apply a tight pressure dressing to the neck.
C. Measure the neck with a tape measure for documentation.
D. Offer ice chips to soothe the throat.
Key: A
Trap: Documentation (C) and Danger Action (B - pressure on carotid/trachea is dangerous).
Filter: Sudden change = act now. This is a surgical emergency (hematoma).

Item 21: A nurse is monitoring a patient in labor receiving an Oxytocin infusion. The electronic fetal monitor shows late decelerations.
What is the nurse’s priority action?
A. Increase the Oxytocin rate to speed up delivery and resolve the stress.
B. Document the deceleration pattern and reassess after three more contractions.
C. Turn the patient to the left side and discontinue the Oxytocin infusion.
D. Perform a vaginal exam to check for umbilical cord prolapse.
Key: C
Trap: Complex Unsafe (A) and Delay/Reassessment (B).
Filter: Abnormal + Unstable = Intervene. Late decels indicate placental insufficiency; stop the stimulus (Oxytocin) first.

Item 62: An RN is working with a UAP (Assistant) and an LPN (Practical Nurse). The unit has just received a patient with an unstable pulmonary embolism.

Item 94: During a mass casualty incident, the nurse finds a victim who is not breathing. After opening the airway, the victim remains apneic.
What color tag should the nurse assign?
A. Red (Immediate) - Begin bag-mask ventilation.
B. Black (Deceased/Expectant) - Move to the next victim.

D. Green (Minor) - Ask bystanders to perform CPR.
Key: B
