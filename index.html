Question 1
A patient with a thyroidectomy 4 hours ago develops inspiratory stridor and is leaning forward to breathe. The surgical dressing is dry and intact.

Which is the most appropriate nursing intervention?

A. Place the patient in a supine position to rest the neck muscles.
B. Call for the emergency tracheostomy kit and stay with the patient.
C. Perform a thorough assessment of the posterior neck for drainage.
D. Document the respiratory rate and reassure the patient that swelling is expected.
ANSWER: B
Trap: Delay (C) or Comfort (D).
Stridor indicates an acute airway obstruction. In SCFHS logic, you must act before you document or call for help from a distance. Maintaining the airway is the safety filter.
Question 2
A patient’s heart rate is 145 bpm with a narrow-complex tachycardia on the monitor. The patient is diaphoretic and reports a blood pressure of 78/40 mmHg.

What is the priority nursing action?

A. Prepare for synchronized cardioversion.
B. Administer a 500mL bolus of Normal Saline.
C. Perform a 12-lead ECG to analyze the rhythm in detail.
D. Document the vital signs and re-check in 15 minutes.
ANSWER: A
Trap: Complexity Trap (C) or Monitoring Trap (D).
Abnormal + Unstable (hypotension) = Immediate Intervention. In Prometric, if the patient is unstable with tachycardia, synchronized cardioversion is the "Safety Filter."
Question 3
During a shift change, the nurse notes that a patient’s IV bag contains Potassium Chloride 40 mEq in 100mL of Saline, which is infusing over 30 minutes.

What should the nurse do first?

A. Notify the physician regarding the medication error.
B. Check the patient's most recent serum potassium lab results.
C. Stop the infusion immediately and disconnect the tubing.
D. Slow the infusion to the prescribed rate and document the error.
ANSWER: C
Trap: Authority Trap (A) or Documentation Trap (D).
Safety Filter: Hold/Stop unsafe meds immediately. Rapid infusion of concentrated potassium can cause cardiac arrest. You must stop the harm before communicating.
Question 4
A patient who is 1 day post-appendectomy is found with a respiratory rate of 8 breaths/min. The patient is currently receiving Morphine via a PCA pump.

Which action should the nurse take first?

A. Administer oxygen via a non-rebreather mask at 10L/min.
B. Stop the PCA pump and attempt to arouse the patient.
C. Call the Rapid Response Team and wait for the code cart.
D. Check the patient's oxygen saturation with pulse oximetry.
ANSWER: B
Trap: Monitoring Trap (D) - waiting for a number.
In opioid-induced respiratory depression, the safety filter is to remove the cause (Stop PCA) and stimulate the patient. Oxygen is secondary to stopping the toxin.
Question 5
A patient with a femoral fracture suddenly becomes restless and develops petechiae on the chest and neck. The SpO2 is 88%.

Which intervention is the priority?

A. Elevate the affected extremity and check for a pulse.
B. Initiate high-flow oxygen and notify the surgeon.
C. Administer the prescribed anticoagulant medication.
D. Document the findings and prepare for an X-ray of the femur.
ANSWER: B
Trap: Delay/Documentation (D).
Petechiae + Respiratory distress after long bone fracture = Fat Embolism. The safety filter is immediate oxygenation.
